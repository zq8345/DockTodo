How it works

  1. Give every task a rate.

    Add a client, a project, an hourly rate. Every task you create underneath inherits them. From that moment, a task isn't a checkbox — it's a line item waiting to happen.

  2. Work with the timer running.

    Start a focus session on any task. When the session ends, DockTodo writes a billable time entry — client, task, duration, dollar amount — with zero extra keystrokes. You never "do timekeeping" as a second job — the entry was written while you worked.

  3. Invoice on Friday.

    The weekly timesheet totals hours and money per client automatically. Pick a client, generate a clean invoice, print to PDF, send it. What used to be an afternoon of archaeology takes about four minutes.

FAQ

Is my data really private?

Yes, by construction rather than by promise. DockTodo runs entirely in your browser; your clients, rates, hours, and invoice amounts are stored locally on your device and never sent anywhere. We don't operate a server that holds your data, so there is nothing on our end to hack, subpoena, or quietly monetize.

What happens if I clear my browser data?

Honest answer: local-first means the data lives in your browser's storage, so wiping that storage deletes your data — same as deleting a file on your desktop. That's why export is one click and plain JSON; make it a habit after invoicing each week and you always have a complete backup. When Pro ships, end-to-end-encrypted sync and versioned encrypted backups will add off-device copies for people who want a belt with their suspenders.

How is this different from Toggl / Moxie / Bonsai?

Those are cloud suites: accounts, servers, your billing data on their infrastructure, and $9–49 a month for a bundle you use a third of. DockTodo is one local tool built around a single mechanism — the focus timer writes your billable entries — so the hours you track and the lines you invoice are the same record, with no export step in between. It opens instantly, works offline, and the core app costs $0.

Is the free plan really free forever?

Yes. The free app runs on your device, so serving it costs us close to nothing — there's no per-user infrastructure bill forcing us to squeeze you later. Pro — encrypted sync and backups, plus the expanded billing features — is the paid product and funds development. Free is the foundation, not the bait.

Can I use it on my phone, or on two computers?

Today, DockTodo is a single-device web app — it works in a mobile browser, but there are no native apps and we're not promising any. Multi-device sync (end-to-end encrypted) is exactly what Pro is for, and it isn't built yet, which is why the Pro button says "waitlist" and not "buy."

What happens to my data if DockTodo shuts down?

Nothing — and that's the point of local-first. The app runs on your machine and keeps working offline whether or not our domain exists; your data was never on our servers to begin with. Your JSON export is readable by anything, so the worst-case scenario is "you have a fast, free tool that no longer gets updates."
